## Design Context

### Users
Cabinet ministers and senior civil servants in the Government of Jamaica. They open this during cabinet meetings, on laptops, in well-lit conference rooms. The job: quickly assess whether the Ministry of Finance budget is executing on track, spot risks before they escalate, and identify which head officers to question. They are busy, non-technical, and need clarity over decoration.

### Brand Personality
Authoritative, structured, warm. This is a Caribbean government instrument — it carries institutional gravity but not the coldness of a Western fintech dashboard. Think "a bound budget report printed on good paper" rather than "a Silicon Valley analytics tool."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Institutional warmth. Clean data hierarchy with generous whitespace, tempered by the vibrancy of Jamaica's national colors (black, green, gold). Not sterile; not festive.
- **Theme**: Light mode. Ministers read this in daylit rooms. Dark mode would fight the environment.
- **References**: UK Treasury dashgets (structural rigor), Jamaican coat of arms (palette authority), printed government gazettes (typography).
- **Anti-references**: Fintech dashboards with cyan-on-dark. Generic SaaS admin panels. Glassmorphism. Purple gradients. Anything that could be a Stripe clone.
- **Colors**: Jamaica national flag — black (#1A1A1A), green (#009B3A), gold (#FFC423). Adapted for data visualization and accessibility. Neutrals tinted toward the green hue for subconscious cohesion.

### Design Principles
1. **Data first**: Every pixel earns its place by communicating budget status. No decoration for decoration's sake.
2. **Hierarchy through typography, not containers**: Use type scale and whitespace to create structure. Not every datum needs a card.
3. **Jamaican identity without kitsch**: The palette is derived from the flag but applied with institutional restraint. Gold is the accent, not the wallpaper.
4. **Transparency by design**: Every status badge is backed by explicit derivation logic, visible on hover. No opaque judgments.
5. **Progressive density**: Level 0 is spacious and scannable. Level 3 is dense and information-rich. The interface breathes at the top and compresses as you drill down.
